Postdoctoral research at the Structural Genomics Consortium, University of Oxford

Blog Publication
Investigating the structure of P73, a close relative of P53, and an activator protein called ASPP2. P53 is a 'tumour suppressor' protein, known as the "guardian of the genome" because of its huge importance in preventing the development of cancer.
Blog Publication Publication Publication
Investigating the structure of an E3 ligase complex of great interest as a cancer drug target. E3 ligases are a key part of the ubiquitin cellular signalling system, in particular the ubiquitin proteasome system, which marks proteins for destruction.
Blog Publication Publication
Study of a group of receptor tyrosine kinases (RTKs) that are of interest as drug targets for several diseases, but particularly cancer. Receptor tyrosine kinases are proteins that relay signals from the outside of the cell to inside.
Blog Blog Publication Blog Publication
Studies on several kinases and their inhibitors. Kinases are signalling proteins and important drug targets for many diseases.
